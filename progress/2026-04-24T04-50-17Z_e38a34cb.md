PR #1725) is blocked on merge conflict per `coordination orient` at

**Structural observation.** The archive-level (§E.1) and per-entry
(§E.2) granularities have different shapes:

1. **Archive-level checks fire once**, before any entry loop, and
   fail fast on a CD-disagrees-with-EOCD or EOCD-internal
   inconsistency. Their site is `parseCentralDir` head (`:321–:323`
   for disk-number) or `parseCentralDir` tail (`:377` for
   `totalEntries`, after the CD walk produces the parsed count).
2. **Per-entry checks fire inside `readEntryData`**, once per
   extracted entry, and fail on a CD-disagrees-with-LH
   inconsistency for that entry. Their site is structured by the
   `usesDataDescriptor` gate (bit 3) — fields that legitimately
   live in the data descriptor (comp/uncomp/crc) are gated; fields
   that must match the CD unconditionally (method, flags, version,
   name-bytes) are not.

Both granularities share the "equality to some authoritative
value" shape, but the authoritative value differs: for
archive-level it is `0` (single-disk invariant) or the
CD-walk-parsed count (vs EOCD-declared); for per-entry it is the
CD-side field (vs LH-side). This split will continue to widen as
#1744 / #1745 / #1746 / PR #1743 land their archive-level checks
and PR #1725 lands its per-entry check.
