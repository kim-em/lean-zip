### E.3 Peer-parser confirmation — INFORMATIONAL

CPython's `zipfile._EndRecData64` reads
`versionNeededToExtract` from the EOCD64 record but does NOT
enforce an upper-bound on it — the field is used for feature
gating only (e.g. raising `NotImplementedError` on
`compress_type` not in the supported set). Go's `archive/zip`
behaves similarly: the field is read into `directoryEnd` but
no upper-bound validation is performed against APPNOTE's spec
ceiling.

This makes PR #1852 a **strict-reader** guard — lean-zip
rejects where CPython / Go accept (until the accepted archive
fails later during feature dispatch). This is the correct
posture for a parser-differential hardening: a tight upper
bound that rejects attacker-smuggled beyond-spec metadata at
the earliest opportunity. The sibling #1856 (EOCD64
archive-layout invariant) explicitly aligns with CPython on
its invariant; this PR's guard is stricter than both CPython
and Go, and the rationale block at `:364-376` correctly
frames the check as beyond-spec-smuggling rejection rather
than peer-parser alignment.
