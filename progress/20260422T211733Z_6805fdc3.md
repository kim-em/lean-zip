## A. Tactic elegance and minimality

The proof body of `Spec.checksum_pair` is 29 LOC (lines 199–227 of
`Zip/Spec/Crc32.lean`). Each cleanup lead from the issue checked in
turn; none produced a usable saving.

**A.1 `hidx₁` collapse via `generalize` + `bv_decide`.** The `hidx₁`
block (lines 208–216) is the verbatim index-simplification chain from
`_singleton`'s `hidx` (lines 171–179), with `b₁` for `b`. The
concluding `bv_decide` at `_singleton`:183 lives on the *outer*
`0xFF000000 ^^^ T = T ^^^ 0xFF000000` re-association — not on the
inner `hidx` goal, which is a mixed-type equation
`(... : UInt32).toNat = ... ^^^ b.toNat : Nat`. `bv_decide` cannot
close it directly: the RHS `b₁.toNat : Nat` sits outside the BitVec
abstraction, and `UInt32.ofNat b₁.toNat` is abstracted as opaque (see
`lean-uint-bitvec` SKILL §"bv_decide fails when expressions contain
`UInt32.ofNat x.toNat`"). The 7-line `rw` chain is the established
lean-4.29.1 route for this shape — keeping parity with `_singleton`.
No saving.

**A.2 Shared `dif_pos` rewrite.** The two `dif_pos` rewrites
(`rw [dif_pos hlt₁]` at line 206; `rw [dif_pos hlt₂]` at line 227)
are separated by three intervening rewrites that re-enable a second
`crcByteTable` unfolding (lines 217–222). Neither `simp only
[crcByteTable, dif_pos hlt₁, dif_pos hlt₂]` nor a paired `have ⟨hlt₁,
hlt₂⟩` works because `hlt₂`'s statement mentions
`mkTable[0xFF ^^^ b₁.toNat]`, which does not exist as a subterm of
the goal until *after* the inner bridge has fired. The two rewrites
are structurally staged. No saving.

**A.3 `0xFFFFFFFF >>> 8 = 0x00FFFFFF` `show`.** Line 219 rewrites the
running-state reduct into the `0x00FFFFFF` literal that the theorem
statement pins via `let s₁`. Tried dropping the `show` by restructuring
the theorem statement to use `0xFFFFFFFF >>> 8` in place of
`0x00FFFFFF`: elaboration succeeds, but the resulting public-API
equation exposes a reducible shift expression that no downstream user
would want as the canonical form. The `let s₁`-bound literal is
load-bearing at the statement level (see §C). The `rfl`-witnessed
`show` is the cost of hiding the reduction inside the `let`. Keep.

**A.4 `hb1 : b₁.toNat < UInt32.size` bridge.** `UInt32.toNat_ofNat_of_lt'`
in Lean 4.29.1 takes a `< UInt32.size` bound; `b₁.toNat_lt` gives
`< 256`. The transit `have hb1 : b₁.toNat < UInt32.size :=
Nat.lt_trans b₁.toNat_lt (by decide)` is the minimum glue. Replacing
with `by omega` fails (`omega` doesn't know `UInt32.size = 2^32` as a
numeric literal relation without help); inlining via
`(Nat.lt_trans b₁.toNat_lt (by decide))` at the use site adds one
line and loses the name. Keep.

**Conclusion:** proof is minimal for the current infrastructure. LOC
delta in `Zip/*`: **+0** this review.

## C. Statement cleanliness

The `let s₁ : UInt32 := ...` at line 193 is load-bearing, confirmed
on the Spec side as the issue requested. Without the `let`, the RHS
mentions `mkTable[0xFF ^^^ b₁.toNat]'(proof)` three separate times
(once as the head XOR term, twice inside the second index
`((s₁ ^^^ b₂_ext) &&& 0xFF).toNat`). Elaborating three copies of the
`(by exact xor_ff_byte_lt_mkTable_size b₁)` proof obligation hits
`maximum recursion depth`; the `let` binds the running state once and
the elaborator sees one proof term. This is the same elaboration
limit flagged by the commit message on the Native side — it
applies to both Spec and Native statements.

Comparison with `Adler32.Spec.checksum_pair`: the Adler-32 RHS is a
single `UInt32.ofNat ((1 + b₁.toNat + b₂.toNat) + ...)` Nat
computation with no embedded proof terms, so no `let` is needed.
CRC32's table-access RHS is genuinely more complex — `let` is
unavoidable.

Identical to `crc32_singleton` (lines 105–110) with `[b]` → `[b₁, b₂]`

**GF(2)[x] blocker annotations** (per review #1697's conclusions,
unchanged by this review):

- `_replicate_zero`: depends on an additive-scalar-action
  characterisation of `crcByte 0` that CRC32 does not admit over
  `UInt32` with the current table-lookup machinery — requires the
  polynomial-ring view where `crcByte (·) 0` = "multiply by x⁸ mod
  POLY", which is a first GF(2)[x] fact.
- `_replicate`: same blocker, extended to arbitrary byte.
- `_combine`: zlib's reference `crc32_combine` uses 32-bit matrix
  exponentiation over GF(2); requires GF(2)[x] polynomial
  multiplication infrastructure.

These three rungs will remain ✗ until a dedicated `meditate`-sized
plan introduces polynomial-ring infrastructure (analogous to the
Adler-32 "GF(p)[x] / modular-polynomial characterisation" future
work noted in the `#1699` closure entry). Downstream planners must
not auto-generate issues for them from the ladder shape.

**Parallel Adler-32 status (unchanged by this review, for
comparison):** Adler-32 ladder closed at #1699 (all seven rungs
landed + audited, including `_combine`). The asymmetry — Adler-32
`_combine` lands without polynomial algebra, CRC32 `_combine` does
not — is structural: Adler operates in `(ℤ/M, ℤ/M)` with linear
byte contributions; CRC32 operates over `GF(2)[x]` where the combine
family requires polynomial algebra.
