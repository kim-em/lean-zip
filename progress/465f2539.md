### Observations on the two-block composition theorems (lines 2783-4035)

The 5 groups of two-block compressed composition theorems follow an
extremely formulaic structure. Each proof body has ~30 lines of "block 1
setup" (parseBlockHeader_succeeds → field extraction → constraint
derivation → subst → rw step → exact single-block) that is nearly
identical across groups. However, extraction is difficult because:
- Each group uses different step theorems (raw_step vs rle_step vs
  compressed_literals_only_step vs compressed_sequences_step)
- The hypothesis sets differ (raw needs hraw, RLE needs hrle, compressed
  needs hlit + hseq + hfse + hbbr + hdec + hexec)
- The proof bodies are short enough (~30 lines) that extraction would
  add complexity without significant savings

The `WellFormedBlocks` inductive predicate (lines 2870-3027) and its
`decompressBlocksWF_succeeds_of_well_formed` theorem (lines 3029-3089)
are well-structured — the induction proof cleanly maps each constructor
to the corresponding step/base theorem.
