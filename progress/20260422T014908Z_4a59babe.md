## What landed

- New `#### Allocation site audit (c/zlib_ffi.c)` subsection in
  `SECURITY_INVENTORY.md` under *"zlib via C FFI"*. Enumerates all 14
  `malloc`/`realloc`/`calloc`/`grow_buffer` call sites (10 direct
  alloc-function mentions + 4 `grow_buffer` call sites) with Site,
  Function, Bound, Failure handling, and Notes columns.
- Summary below the table records what the `grow_buffer`-centred
  pattern catches (overflow doubling, alloc failure, double-free,
  over-4 GiB whole-buffer inputs) and what it does NOT catch
  (decompression bombs with `max_output == 0` can still walk the
  buffer up to `SIZE_MAX/2`; streaming entry points have no output
  cap; zlib internal allocations are outside this audit).
- Replaced the bare "audit all formatted error and allocation helpers
  after any C changes" `Missing work` bullet with a sharper invariant
  that points at the new subsection and the helper script.
- Ticked Priority 3 item 4 in
  `plans/track-e-current-audit-checklist.md` with a pointer back to
  the inventory subsection; explicitly kept the re-audit-on-change
  invariant as the rule the ticked item governs.
- New `scripts/check-c-allocations.sh` (~45 lines with `--help`): runs
  `grep -cE 'malloc|realloc|calloc' c/zlib_ffi.c`, compares against a
  baseline (`EXPECTED=10`) baked at the top, and prints one of:
  - clean match line, or
  - a DRIFT warning with the current `grep -nE` listing and a pointer
    to re-run the audit. Always exits 0 — advisory only, not wired
    into CI in this PR per the issue's explicit scope.
