## Accomplished

- **`Zip/Spec/Adler32.lean` — `checksum_combine`.** Proved the two-block
  closed form for Adler-32:

      checksum (xs ++ ys)
        = pack ((a1 + b1 + M - 1) % M,
                (a2 + n·a1 + b2 + n·M - n) % M)

  where `(a1, a2) = updateList init xs`, `(b1, b2) = updateList init ys`,
  `n = ys.length`, `M = MOD_ADLER`. Matches zlib's `adler32_combine`
  formula and is the genuine characterizing property (`checksum_append`
  only splits, not combines).

- **`Zip/Native/Adler32.lean` — `adler32_combine` + `adler32_combine_eq_concat`.**
  Computable combine function mirroring the spec formula, plus correctness
  theorem bridging through `updateBytes_eq_updateList`.

- **`ZipTest/NativeChecksum.lean` — runtime conformance test.** Four
  `(xs, ys)` pairs of distinct lengths (including empty and ~10-byte
  second blocks) asserting `adler32_combine ax ay ys.size = adler32 1 (xs ++ ys)`.

## Proof strategy

The combine formula cannot be derived directly from `checksum_append`
(which expresses `checksum (xs ++ ys)` in terms of `checksum xs` by
threading bytes through, not by a byte-free combine on individual
packed checksums). Instead, I introduced a **separation formula**:

    updateList (a, b) xs = ((a + S(xs)) % M,
                            (b + |xs|·a + T(xs)) % M)

where `S` is the linear byte sum and `T` is the triangular-weighted
sum. This is provable by simple left-induction (no reverse induction
needed). Applying the separation formula with both `(1, 0)` and `(a, b)`
starting states then gives the state-level combine lemma
`updateList_combine`, which plus `checksum_append` yields
`checksum_combine`.

Three small private mod-manipulation helpers (`mod_subst_mul`,
`mod_subst_mul_middle`, `mod_absorb_leading`) absorb the `% M`
from subterms so `omega` can finish the inner Nat polynomial equalities.

## Decisions

- **Dropped the `len2 % MOD_ADLER` optimization** mentioned in the issue
  body. It was "cheap modular reduction" but introduces a second proof
  obligation (bridging `len2 % M` vs `len2` in the Nat-subtraction form)
  that isn't worth the tiny runtime saving. `adler32_combine` uses
  `len2` directly; the trailing `% MOD_ADLER` at each component already
  handles reduction.

- **Used `.1`/`.2` projections** for `checksum_combine`'s statement
  rather than the issue's `let ⟨a1, a2⟩` destructuring. Proofs are
  cleaner with explicit projections, and the user-facing formula is
  unchanged modulo cosmetics.
