## Observations

### Most consequential change: the three-repo split (#1487)

PR #1487 ended a three-month period in which this repository held all
three pillars (DEFLATE, Zstd, and shared utilities) in one tree. The
split has three visible effects going forward:

1. **Scope narrowing**: `lean-zip` now focuses on DEFLATE + zlib +
   gzip + ZIP/Tar archives. Anything Zstd-related in new planner issues
   should be filed against `lean-zstd` instead.
2. **Dependency coupling**: the `zipCommon from git` pin couples this
   repo's green master to a specific commit of `lean-zip-common`. PRs
   #1499 and #1506 already demonstrate the bump-and-reconfigure
   workflow when upstream changes lemma names or internal structure.
3. **Progress-metric discontinuity**: the PROGRESS.md Current-State
   block is now a mixed artifact — older entries describe infrastructure
   (FSE, XxHash, ZstdHuffman, …) that no longer lives here. Future
   summarize passes should either (a) scope metrics to what's currently
   in-tree, or (b) cross-link to the sibling repos' PROGRESS.md files.

### Proven-bounds campaign progress

Pre-batch: 149 `]!` across 10 Native/common/shared files.
Post-batch (accounting for the split): 50 `]!` remain, all in two
DEFLATE Native files. The Zstd-side `]!` conversions (#1426, #1428,
#1475, #1482, #1447, and #1433 for BitReader) are now tracked in
`lean-zstd` or `lean-zip-common` respectively.

The campaign's remaining work in *this* repo (post-split) is the LZ77
matcher cluster in `Deflate.lean`, explicitly carved into three feature
issues (#1508/#1509/#1510) plus the DeflateDynamic.lean residual
(#1505).

### Toolchain regression

Lean 4.29.1 introduced a behavioural change where `lake exe test` can
report a "compiled configuration invalid" error on CI even though a
fresh `lake test` passes locally. The two fix PRs (#1495 renaming the
CI invocation, then #1506 adding `lake reconfigure` and pinning
zipCommon) bracket the regression. This is worth remembering for
future toolchain bumps: the lakefile now does a git-dependency
`reconfigure` dance that is sensitive to Lake's config cache.

### Scope-creep check

One PR — #1497 — is the only "new DEFLATE feature" merged in the batch:
it adds local-header span validation in `Archive.readEntryData` before
`Handle.read`. Everything else is bookkeeping, proven-bounds, or
refactoring. In other words, for DEFLATE proofs themselves this batch
is **read-mostly** — the major movement is the structural split and
the runtime-bounds cleanup.
