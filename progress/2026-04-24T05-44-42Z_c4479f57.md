## Summary

Added the reader-side check for the ZIP64 EOCD64 record's self-declared
`size of zip64 end of central directory record` field (APPNOTE §4.3.14).
The field at `bufPos + 4` is now read and must equal exactly `44` for
the v1 EOCD64 shape lean-zip produces and consumes.  Closes the
parser-differential smuggling vector where lean-zip (fixed 56-byte
layout) silently accepts a crafted length, while a stricter parser
that trusts the self-declared length would read past or short of the
expected 56 bytes.

## Decisions made

- **Strictness**: stuck with `== 44` (option (a) in the issue).  Smoke
  test shows Go produces `44`; no real-world producer evidence to the
  contrary on the lean-zip interop corpus.  If Info-ZIP or another
  producer is later encountered emitting `56` or an extensible-data-
  sector record, relax to `>= 44` or `== 44 || == 56` as a follow-up.
- **Return-type of `findEndOfCentralDir`**: already `IO (Option ...)`
  by the time this session started (landed via prior Track E PRs).
  The new `throw` slots in directly.
- **Fixture generation**: (b) — extended the PR #1745 generator
  additively rather than patching `go-zip64.zip`.  The generator was
  already in-tree; reuse keeps both ZIP64 malformed fixtures byte-
  reproducible from a single script.  Default `eocd64_record_size=44`
  preserves byte-identity of the sibling fixture.
- **Placement of the check inside the ZIP64 branch**: placed it
  *before* the override-sentinel checks so the record-size vector can
  be closed independently of the override-mismatch vector (matters if
  a crafted archive carries both smuggling shapes — each fires its own
  error).  Confirmed by running the test fixture.
