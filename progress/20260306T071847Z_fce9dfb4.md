## Accomplished

Proved two frame-level characterizing properties of `decompressFrame` in
`Zip/Spec/Zstd.lean`:

1. **`decompressFrame_contentSize_eq`**: When the frame header specifies
   `contentSize = some n`, the decompressed output has exactly `n` bytes.

2. **`decompressFrame_checksum_valid`**: When the frame header has
   `contentChecksum = true`, the output's XXH64 upper 32 bits matches
   the checksum stored at `pos' - 4`.

Both are characterizing properties (level 2 specs) — they relate the output
to frame header metadata and a cryptographic hash, not just restating the
implementation.

## Proof approach

Both proofs follow the same 4-step pattern:
1. `unfold decompressFrame` + `dsimp [Bind.bind, Except.bind]` to expose the do-block
2. `rw [hh]` to substitute the known `parseFrameHeader` result
3. `simp only [hn]` (or `[hc]`) to resolve the relevant match/conditional
4. `grind` to thread through the remaining guard chain (dictionary ID,
   decompressBlocks, checksum guards, content size guard)

`grind` handles the large case tree (2 dictionary branches × 2 decompressBlocks
outcomes × multiple if-guards) automatically. This was significantly simpler
than the manual `by_cases`/`split at h` approach initially attempted.
