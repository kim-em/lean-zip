## Findings per deliverable

1. **`SpanInFile` predicate form (overflow safety).** Confirmed the
   `length ‚Č§ fileSize - offset` form is correct. Verified in
   `Init/Data/UInt/Basic.lean:577` that `UInt64.sub` wraps modularly
   (`BitVec.sub`), NOT saturates ‚ÄĒ so on its own the second conjunct is
   not sound. The predicate's correctness depends on the first conjunct
   `offset ‚Č§ fileSize` guarding against wraparound. Once that holds,
   `fileSize - offset` has its intended meaning. The progress entry
   for PR #1636 described this as "saturating"; more precisely it is
   "wrap-safe conditional on the left conjunct". The existing
   docstring on `SpanInFile` is already careful ("saturating remainder
   (well-defined once `offset ‚Č§ fileSize`)") and does not need fixing.
   Existing five `by decide` examples cover happy / offset-overshoot /
   span-overshoot / empty-head / tail-boundary; none exercise the
   `UInt64.max` wrap boundary. Landed one overflow-regression example
   pinning the divergence between `length ‚Č§ fileSize - offset` and the
   alternative `offset + length ‚Č§ fileSize` formulation.

2. **Proof minimality.**
   - Forward reduction (`assertSpanInFile_eq_pure_of_spanInFile`):
     3-line `unfold` + two `rw [if_neg ...]` + `rfl` is already tight.
     Tried to collapse to `simp only [assertSpanInFile, if_neg]` but
     simp cannot infer the negations from `h.1 / h.2` without the
     explicit `UInt64.not_lt.mpr` bridge. Left as-is.
   - Backward reduction (`spanInFile_of_assertSpanInFile_succeeds`):
     the original proof duplicated the "refute `¬¨ offset ‚Č§ fileSize`"
     branch ‚ÄĒ once as the first conjunct goal, once re-derived inside
     the second conjunct's `have h1 : offset ‚Č§ fileSize`. Hoisted `h1`
     out of `refine ‚ü®?_, ?_‚ü©`: prove `h1` first, then supply it to
     both `refine ‚ü®h1, ?_‚ü©` and the second-guard contradiction.
     Saves 3 lines and reads more linearly.
