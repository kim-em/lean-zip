## Accomplished

Added compression benchmarks to `ZipTest/NativeScale.lean`:

1. **`testCompressMatrix`**: Native vs FFI raw deflate compression throughput across
   all 4 patterns × 4 sizes (1KB–1MB) × 3 levels (0, 1, 6). Includes roundtrip
   verification (decompress native output with FFI) and MB/s throughput. 1MB timing
   entries feed into the summary section.

2. **`testCompressQuality`**: Compression quality comparison at 1MB for prng and text
   patterns across all 10 levels (0–9). Shows native output size, FFI output size, and
   native/FFI ratio.

## Key observations from benchmark data

- **Level 0 (stored)**: Native and FFI produce identical output (ratio 1.0).
- **Incompressible data (prng)**: Native is ~5% larger than FFI at levels 1–4 (ratio 1.054),
  nearly equal at levels 5–9 (ratio 1.0003).
- **Compressible data (text)**: Native output is 1.5–2.4× larger than FFI. The gap widens
  at higher levels, suggesting the native lazy matcher doesn't yet match zlib's more
  aggressive search at levels 6+.
- **Throughput**: Native compression runs ~5–125 MB/s depending on level and pattern.
  FFI (zlib) is ~15–30× faster, as expected for optimized C vs interpreted Lean.
