## Scope

PR #1631 raises the `maxDecompressedSize` default from `0` (unlimited)
to `1 * 1024 * 1024 * 1024` (1 GiB) on the three streaming FFI
decompression entry points, closing out SECURITY_INVENTORY Rec. 2.
With PR #1623 (whole-buffer Rec. 1) already landed, the entire public
FFI decompression surface now shares a single 1 GiB default.

This review audits (a) default consistency with the whole-buffer flip,
(b) docstring-template parity, (c) regression-test coverage, and
(d) `SECURITY_INVENTORY.md` + checklist reconciliation. It does not
re-audit the cap-enforcement branch itself (covered by PR #1616 for
the parameter-addition half).

## Audit findings

- `Gzip.decompressStream` at [`Zip/Gzip.lean:87`](/home/kim/lean-zip/Zip/Gzip.lean:87):
  default `1024 * 1024 * 1024`. ✓
- `Gzip.decompressFile` at [`Zip/Gzip.lean:128`](/home/kim/lean-zip/Zip/Gzip.lean:128):

All six FFI decompression entry points (whole-buffer + streaming +

Observation — `Gzip.decompressFile` re-specifies its own 1 GiB default
at [`Zip/Gzip.lean:128`](/home/kim/lean-zip/Zip/Gzip.lean:128) and
forwards the parameter value to `decompressStream` at
[`Zip/Gzip.lean:136-137`](/home/kim/lean-zip/Zip/Gzip.lean:136). The
two defaults are independent literals. A future change to the
`decompressStream` default would not automatically propagate to
`decompressFile`. The compile-time rfl probe at
[`ZipTest/Gzip.lean:12-13`](/home/kim/lean-zip/ZipTest/Gzip.lean:12)
pins `@Gzip.decompressFile p none (1024 * 1024 * 1024)` by `rfl`, so a
silent drift would fail elaboration — the drift risk is caught at
test-compile time even though the source has two literals. Acceptable
as-is.
