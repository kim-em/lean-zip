## Key decision: inlined two-step proofs

The block-level composition theorems (`decompressBlocksWF_compressed_seq_then_compressed_lit`
and `decompressBlocksWF_compressed_lit_then_compressed_seq`) have a dependent-type issue
at their `hlit2` parameter: Lean's elaboration of `if let some ht := huffTree1 then some ht else prevHuff`
creates a match that depends on the earlier `hlit1` proof parameter. When instantiating
`prevHuff := none` at frame level, the match motives differ between the theorem's context
and the frame-level context, causing a type mismatch despite identical-looking types.

**Solution**: Instead of calling the composition theorems directly, the frame-level proofs
inline the two-step approach (`rw [step_theorem]` then `exact single_block_theorem`).
The frame-level hypotheses use `huffTree1` directly for block 2's `parseLiteralsSection`
(simpler interface), with `(by cases huffTree1 <;> exact hlit2)` bridging the gap at
single-block theorem call sites.
