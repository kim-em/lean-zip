### A.3 Smuggled value choice: `linkname = "evil.lnk\x00.tar"` (13 B printable + interior NUL)

A direct `xxd` of the 512 B header in
`testdata/tar/malformed/ustar-linkname-nul-in-name.tar`:

| Slice                    | Offset       | Size  | Content |
|--------------------------|--------------|-------|---------|
| `name`                   | `0x000..0x063` | 100 B | `"safe"` (4 B printable) + 96 B NUL padding — **clean** |
| `mode` ... `mtime`       | `0x064..0x093` | 48 B  | octal numeric fields (writer-emitted) |
| `chksum`                 | `0x094..0x09B` | 8 B   | `"011024\x00 "` (recomputed by writer to match the smuggled `linkname`) |
| `typeflag`               | `0x09C`        | 1 B   | `'0'` (REGTYPE — regular file) |
| `linkname`               | `0x09D..0x100` | 100 B | **`"evil.lnk\x00.tar"`** (13 B with interior NUL at byte 8) + 87 B NUL padding |
| `magic`                  | `0x101..0x106` | 6 B   | `"ustar\x00"` — UStar branch entered |
| `version`                | `0x107..0x108` | 2 B   | `"00"` |
| `uname` ... `prefix`     | `0x109..0x1FF` | 247 B | NUL-padded; `prefix` slot at offset 345 / 155 B all-NUL — **clean** |

Header inspection confirms the three slot-pinning conditions:

- **`name = "safe"` is NUL-free** (the smuggled NUL is in
  `linkname`, not `name`) — so the line-:514/515 sub-check at
  `hasInteriorNul block hdrName.1 hdrName.2` evaluates `false`,
  the guard does NOT fire on the `name` arm, and control reaches
  the `linkname` sub-check at :516/517.
- **`linkname = "evil.lnk\x00.tar"` carries an interior NUL at
  byte 8** (between `'k'` and `'.'`, before the trailing NUL
  padding starts) — the `hasInteriorNul block hdrLinkname.1
  hdrLinkname.2` test at :516 evaluates `true`, the throw at :517
  fires with substring `"UStar linkname contains NUL byte"`, and
  control never reaches the `prefix` sub-check at :518/519.
- **`prefix` is all-NUL (clean)** — even if the throw at :517 had
  not fired, the `prefix` arm would not have tripped either. So
  the `linkname` arm is the **uniquely** tripped sub-check for
  this fixture.

The choice of `"evil.lnk\x00.tar"` is well-defended:

- **13 printable-ASCII bytes** (`evil.lnk`, `.tar` separated by an
  interior NUL) — symmetrically shaped to PR #1880's `name` slot
  smuggle `"evil\x00.tar"` (13 bytes there too) and to PR #1937's
  `prefix` slot smuggle `"badpfx\x00bad"` (10 bytes). The interior
  NUL position is offset 8 inside the 13-byte payload — strictly
  *interior*, not a trailing padding NUL.
- **Interior-NUL position chosen between two distinct file
  extensions** (`.lnk` and `.tar`) — exemplifies the
  parser-differential / filesystem-truncation smuggle: pre-PR,
  `Binary.readString` would truncate `linkname` at the NUL to
  `"evil.lnk"`, while strict peer parsers would either preserve
  the full 13-byte form or reject. POSIX `open(2)` on
  `Tar.extract` truncates at NUL, so `entry.linkname = "evil.lnk"`
  in the lean-zip output would not match strict-peer output —
  a classic smuggle vector.
- **Distinct from the `name` arm's smuggle** (`"evil\x00.tar"`,
  12 bytes including NUL) — the `.lnk` infix marks the slot
  identity in `xxd` diffs across the family.
- **Fits well within the 100-byte `linkname` field** (13 of 100
  bytes used; 87 B of NUL padding follows) — no field-overrun
  hazard.

The smuggled value's `linkname` slot location (offset 157 / 100 B)
is the middle slot of the 3-slot family; sibling slots are `name`
at offset 0 / 100 B (PR #1880) and `prefix` at offset 345 / 155 B
(PR #1937). All three slots are read by `Binary.readString`
post-:514-519 guard at :520-:531.

## F. Reviewer observations

- **F.1 Per-slot-distinct substring discipline at the test level.**
  PR #1934's `assertThrows` substring (`"UStar linkname contains
  NUL byte"`) is per-slot-distinct from the `name`-arm assertion
  at :217 (`"UStar name contains NUL byte"`) and from the
  `prefix`-arm assertion at :249 (`"UStar prefix contains NUL
  byte"`). The slot-name suffix is what gives this fixture its
  distinct test-level attribution claim — a test that matched on
  the bare `"UStar"` prefix alone could not distinguish a trip at
  :515 from a trip at :517 from a trip at :519. The per-slot-
  distinct substring discipline is a **superior shape** compared
  to the post-#1894 ZIP64-override family's shared substring
  convention; the latter relies entirely on fixture construction
  for per-slot attribution, while the UStar family uses both.
  See §E.4.e for the catalogue-update flag.

- **F.2 `"safe"` discipline for the `name` slot.**
  The fixture's `name = "safe"` is deliberately a trivial 4-byte
  printable-ASCII payload with no smuggling potential. This
  discipline ensures the `name`-arm guard at :515 cannot fire
  first under any header-parse interpretation — even an attacker
  who somehow controlled the `name` field would see this fixture
  decline the smuggle on that arm. The `"safe"` choice is
  symmetric to PR #1937's `name = "name.txt"` for the prefix-slot
  fixture (also a safe payload that cannot trip the `name` arm).
  The discipline is the test-side analog of the writer-side
  invariant ("`Binary.writeString` is NUL-padding-only"): a
  legitimate archive's `name` field carries a predictable,
  NUL-free payload, and the fixture's smuggle is contained
  strictly in the targeted slot.

- **F.3 Writer-side compliance.** lean-zip's own
  [`Tar.UStar.buildHeader`](/home/kim/lean-zip/Zip/Tar.lean:381)
  emits `linkname` via `Binary.writeString`, which is NUL-padding-
  only — the writer cannot emit an interior NUL in the `linkname`
  slot unless `entry.linkname` carries a literal `\x00` codepoint.
  Writer-side non-smuggle invariant holds (PR #1934 cannot fire on
  a lean-zip-produced archive). Sibling invariant to
  paired-review-#1880 §F.1 (for the `name` slot) and to
  paired-review-#1937 §F.3 (for the `prefix` slot, which has the
  additional `pathOverride := some (...)` caveat for the writer-
  side smuggle vector that PR #1937's fixture exercises directly).

- **F.4 Fixture non-bloat across the family.** The three landed
  family fixtures are all 1 536 B byte-wise — the builder's three
  emit functions are independent and the byte-budget is identical
  per slot. Total Track E fixture-corpus size in
  `testdata/tar/malformed/` grows by 1 536 B per per-slot fixture;
  the full 3-slot family contributes 4 608 B (1 536 × 3). Well
  within the corpus size budget flagged by the
  `malformed-fixture-builder` skill.

- **F.5 Builder script is Lean, not Python.** Unlike the
  ZIP64-override family's `scripts/build-zip64-malformed-fixtures.py`
  (Python with `struct.pack` and direct byte construction), the
  UStar family uses
  [`scripts/build-ustar-malformed-fixtures.lean`](/home/kim/lean-zip/scripts/build-ustar-malformed-fixtures.lean) —
  a Lean script that exercises the in-tree `Tar.UStar.*` writer
  builders directly. This is a stronger byte-determinism guarantee
  (the same writer code that emits legitimate archives also emits
  the malformed fixtures, modulo the smuggled-NUL injection) and a
  stronger writer-side compliance guarantee (writer changes that
  break byte-determinism would surface immediately as fixture
  drift, before they break legitimate archive emission). Worth
  preserving as a precedent for future tar-side malformed-fixture
  families.

- **F.6 Guard-ordering sanity.** A future hardening that combined
  the 3 sub-checks at :514-519 into a single loop over an array of
  slots would need to preserve the per-throw wording and the
  per-slot ordering to keep all three fixtures' `assertThrows`
  substrings valid (and to keep this fixture's attribution
  unambiguous: a refactor that moved the `linkname` arm before
  the `name` arm would not change the trip behavior, but would
  invalidate the §B.1 ordering claim if the test relied on
  short-circuit semantics — it does not, since each fixture's
  `name` slot is NUL-clean by construction). Same observation as
  paired-review-1922 §F.5 for the post-#1894 ZIP64-override
  family.

- **F.7 Per-slot family vs per-slot wave terminology.** The 3-slot
  UStar family is structurally analogous to the post-#1894 6-slot
  ZIP64-override family but is much smaller and was authored over
  a much longer span (6h 37min vs ~2h). The "wave" terminology
  used by the post-#1904 summarize for the ZIP64-override family

  family is better described as a "small per-slot family closure"

  multi-PR wave. The post-#1928 wave summarize (E.4.c) should
