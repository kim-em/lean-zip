## Baseline benchmark numbers

| Size  | Pattern | Level | Native MB/s | FFI MB/s   | Slowdown |
|-------|---------|-------|-------------|------------|----------|
| 16KB  | text    | 1     | 118         | 4,266      | 36x      |
| 16KB  | text    | 6     | 147         | 5,811      | 40x      |
| 64KB  | text    | 1     | 170         | 8,449      | 50x      |
| 64KB  | text    | 6     | 219         | 10,839     | 50x      |
| 256KB | text    | 1     | 185         | 11,058     | 60x      |
| 256KB | text    | 6     | 256         | 16,055     | 63x      |
| *KB   | prng    | *     | 15K–29K     | 31K–38K    | 1–2x     |

Key findings:
- Native decompressor is 36–63x slower than FFI zlib on compressible text data
- The gap widens with larger inputs (cache effects, algorithmic overhead)
- Incompressible data (prng) has minimal gap (~1–2x) since there's nothing to decode
- Native throughput for text data: 118–256 MB/s (usable but not fast)
