## What was accomplished

Created `ZipTest/ZstdDecompressBench.lean` — Zstd decompression throughput
benchmarks following the pattern from `NativeCompressBench.lean`:

- FFI compress at levels 1, 3, 9, 19 → measure native and FFI decompress
- 4 data patterns (constant, cyclic, prng, text) × 4 sizes (1KB–1MB)
- Reports compression ratio, native MB/s, FFI MB/s in aligned table
- Warns (but doesn't fail) on native content mismatch for forward compat
- Registered in `ZipTest.lean`

## Key observations from benchmark output

- Native decompressor achieves 60–330 MB/s on compressible text data (1MB),
  vs FFI at 5000–18000 MB/s — roughly 60–90× slower
- On incompressible (prng) data, native is 6–12 GB/s since decompression
  is trivial (stored blocks)
- Constant data: native ~1 GB/s (RLE blocks), FFI ~17–35 GB/s
- The performance gap is consistent across compression levels
