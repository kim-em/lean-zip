## Key patterns

The 10 bare simps fell into three categories:

1. **Option.bind chain contradictions** (5 instances, lines 136-155):
   `simp [hflc] at hencsym` in `none =>` branches needed the full Option monad
   lemma set: `Option.pure_def, Option.bind_eq_bind, Option.bind_none,
   Option.bind_some, Option.bind_fun_none, reduceCtorEq`.
   Sequential `simp only` applications were order-dependent and could not be
   merged with preceding `simp only` calls.

2. **Array size/indexing bridging** (3 instances, lines 94, 103, 108):
   Simple `simp only` with `Array.length_toList`, `List.drop_eq_nil_iff`,
   `Array.getElem_toList`.

3. **List.map identity cancellation** (2 instances, lines 229, 236):
   After `List.map_congr_left` proves pointwise identity, `simp only
   [List.map_id_fun', id_eq]` closes the map-id goal.
