## D. One-sided design rationale audit

**D.1 Struct field docstring on `Entry.versionNeeded`**
(`Zip/Archive.lean:37–42`):

> CD-side `versionNeededToExtract` field (APPNOTE §4.4.3.2),
> preserved for CD-vs-LH consistency checking.  Writers ignore this
> field and emit a fixed value (`20` for non-ZIP64, `45` for ZIP64);
> the check in `readEntryData` only rejects LH claiming a higher
> version than CD.

Covers the *what*: the check is one-sided, rejects LH > CD only.
Does not spell out the smuggling vector or the producer-evidence
rationale — that lives in the inline comment at the check site. ✓

**D.2 Inline rationale at the check site** (`Zip/Archive.lean:641–647`):

> One-sided CD/LH versionNeededToExtract check.  Rejects LH claiming
> a higher version than CD (a capability-smuggle vector — e.g. LH=45
> "ZIP64 features required" alongside CD=20, bypassing readers that
> feature-gate on the CD).  The converse (CD > LH) is legitimate:
> Go's `archive/zip` and CPython's `zipfile` emit ZIP64 archives
> whose LH sizes fit in 32 bits with `LH.versionNeeded=20` while the
> CD carries a ZIP64 size extra with `CD.versionNeeded=45`.

This is the full rationale: *why* it is one-sided (capability-smuggle
vector on one direction only), *why the converse is legitimate*
(documented Go/CPython producer behaviour), and a concrete example
(LH=45/CD=20 is the attack direction; LH=20/CD=45 is the legitimate
producer pattern). Not framed as a "defensive compromise for
interop"; framed as the **correct formalisation of the smuggling
vector** — matching deliverable 6's intent. ✓

**No drift detected.** The struct field docstring is deliberately
terse ("writers ignore … only rejects LH > CD"); the
smuggling-vector rationale lives at the check site where it is most
relevant to a future reader. This split is consistent with how the
`flags` field is documented (`:33–:35` carries only the bit-3-mask
rationale; the deeper bit-11 smuggling context lives at the flags
check, `:634–:637`). No follow-up issue filed.
