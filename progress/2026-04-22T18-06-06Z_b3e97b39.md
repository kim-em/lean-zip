## Accomplishments

1. Added `Spec.checksum_singleton` in `Zip/Spec/Crc32.lean`:
   `checksum [b] = 0xFF000000 ^^^ mkTable[0xFF ^^^ b.toNat]`. This is
   the CRC32 analog of `Adler32.Spec.checksum_singleton` — the
   single-byte rung of the CRC32 ladder.
2. Added `Native.crc32_singleton` wrapping the spec-level theorem for
   the public `crc32` API (default `init = 0`) via
   `updateBytes_eq_updateList`. Mirrors
   `Native.adler32_singleton` (`Zip/Native/Adler32.lean:48`).
3. Supporting infrastructure in `Zip/Spec/Crc32.lean`:
   - `mkTable_size : mkTable.size = 256` (public)
   - `xor_ff_byte_lt_mkTable_size (b : UInt8) : 0xFF ^^^ b.toNat <
     mkTable.size` (public; shared between Spec and Native)
   - `crcByteTable_mkTable_eq_crcByte` (public) — spec-level bridge
     from the table-driven byte update to the bit-by-bit `crcByte`
   - Private copies of `crcBits8_split`, `UInt32_ofNat_UInt8_toNat`,
     `and_0xFF_toNat_lt`, `xor_byte_shr8`, `mkTable_getElem` — all
     duplicated from `Zip/Native/Crc32.lean` to keep the Spec file
     independent of Native (as requested by the issue).

## Decisions

- The issue explicitly prefers Spec-side independence from Native.
  The `private` helper lemmas are therefore duplicated rather than
  moved upstream — the five helpers are CRC-algorithm-specific bit
  manipulations, not generic enough to extract to
  `lean-zip-common` or to an internal module at this stage.
- The `h`-proof inside the `mkTable[0xFF ^^^ b.toNat]'h` bracket
  requires a `by exact xor_ff_byte_lt_mkTable_size b` wrapper rather
  than a direct term application. Direct term application
  (`xor_ff_byte_lt_mkTable_size b`) triggers a `maximum recursion
  depth` elaboration error — `by exact` defers to tactic elaboration
  and resolves cleanly. Preserved as-is; not worth fighting.
- The final XOR identity is closed by
  `generalize mkTable[0xFF ^^^ b.toNat]'(hidx ▸ hlt) = t` followed
  by `bv_decide`. Without the `generalize`, `bv_decide` sees the two
  `mkTable` accesses (one from the LHS after `getElem_congr_idx`,
  one from the RHS of the theorem) as distinct opaque variables
  because their proof terms differ syntactically. `generalize`
  collapses them via proof irrelevance.
