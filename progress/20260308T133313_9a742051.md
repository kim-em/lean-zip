## Accomplished

1. **`decodeOneSequence`** helper in `Zip/Native/ZstdSequence.lean`:
   Private function extracting symbol lookup + extra bits reading for one
   sequence step. Returns the decoded sequence triple plus the three FSE cells

2. **`decodeSequencesWF.loop`** in `Zip/Native/ZstdSequence.lean`:
   WF variant of the interleaved FSE sequence decoder inner loop. Structural
   recursion on `remaining` count. Manages three FSE states (litLen, matchLen,
   offset) with explicit match on all intermediate `Except` results (no `do`
   notation) for proof-friendly unfolding.

3. **`decodeSequencesWF`** in `Zip/Native/ZstdSequence.lean`:
   Top-level WF wrapper that initializes three FSE states from the backward
   bitstream, then delegates to the loop. Also uses explicit match.

4. **`decodeSequencesWF_loop_size`** in `Zip/Spec/ZstdSequence.lean`:
   When the inner loop succeeds, `result.size = acc.size + remaining`.
   Proved by induction on `remaining` with `split at h` for each match branch.

5. **`decodeSequencesWF_size`** in `Zip/Spec/ZstdSequence.lean`:
   When `decodeSequencesWF` succeeds with `numSeq > 0`, `result.size = numSeq`.

## Decisions

- Extracted `decodeOneSequence` to reduce nesting depth in the WF loop.
  This made the proof tractable: without it, 12+ levels of nested `split at h`
  were needed. With it, the loop body has only ~6 splits.
- Used explicit `match` (not `do` notation) for all monadic operations in the
  WF functions. `do` notation desugars to `Bind.bind` which requires careful
  `simp only [bind, Bind.bind, Except.bind]` management. Explicit match makes
  `split at h` predictable.
- Original `decodeSequences` unchanged (additive change only).
