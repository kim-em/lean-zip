## Changes

### Bare `simp at h` → `exact nomatch h` (37 instances)
All bare `simp at h` calls were used to close contradictory hypotheses
after `split at h`. Since the hypothesis is always a constructor mismatch
(`Except.error _ = Except.ok _`), `exact nomatch h` is the right
replacement — it's more explicit about what's happening and doesn't
depend on the simp set.

### `simp [X] at h` → `simp only [X] at h; exact nomatch h` (17 instances)
Goal-closing `simp [X] at h` calls had two jobs: substitute a lemma
and close the resulting contradiction. Split into `simp only [X] at h`
(substitution) + `exact nomatch h` (contradiction closure).

## Patterns discovered

- `exact nomatch h` is the canonical replacement for bare `simp at h`
  used to close constructor mismatches. It's more robust than
  `contradiction` (which hits max recursion depth in deeply nested contexts)
  and more explicit than bare `simp`.
- For `if false = true then X else Y` patterns, `simp only [if_neg Bool.false_ne_true]`
  evaluates the conditional.
- `simp only [X] at h` does not close constructor inequalities —
  bare `simp` has built-in discriminator analysis that `simp only` lacks.
  Must append `; exact nomatch h` for contradiction closure.
