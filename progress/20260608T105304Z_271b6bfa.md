## Key finding (first real-data read)

The synthetic "ratio parity" is an artifact of the pathologically compressible
`text` pattern. On Canterbury (L6, geomean over 11 files) native is the **worst
real codec on all three axes**: ratio 0.323 (zlib 0.299), compress 10.4 MB/s
(zlib 55), decompress 92 MB/s (zlib 696). The ratio gap is **large on big prose**
— `plrabn12.txt` +30%, `lcet10.txt` +24% vs zlib — the headline next target
(likely single-block Huffman / no block splitting over large files).
